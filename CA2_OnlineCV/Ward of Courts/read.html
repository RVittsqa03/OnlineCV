The General Solicitor

The General Solicitor is appointed by the High Court if there is a perceived conflict of interest; where there are disagreements among the relatives as to how Ward's affairs should be managed; and where there is no suitable relative who is prepared to act. The General Solicitor acts as the Committe of the Estate and assumes the legal identity of the Ward. The General Solicitor has the power to act on Ward's behalf. The General Solicitor can also be appointed by the High Court as the Committee of the Person. In other word, the General Solicitor pays him/herself out of the Ward's fund any salary and expenses as well as his/her general day-to-day Solicitor's role. As he/she can assume the both: Committee of the Person, looking after the Ward's welfare and Committee of the Estate: managing the financial affairs, one would expect an honest, transparent solicitor who follow the professional code of conduct.

The Committee of the Person

The Committee of the Person can be made up of one person or more. That one person can be a solicitor or a team of “experts”. The “experts” are usually from medical background; social workers; solicitors; and specialist doctors ie. Mental Health/Psychiatry. Responsible for the welfare of the Ward. Making sure the Ward's eyes are checked yearly, glasses are replaced, medical appointments kept, monitoring health and medical conditions, ensuring a nutritional balanced diet and healthy element of walking, social trips, personal hygiene and family events etc etc.

The Committee of the Estate

The Committee of the Estate can be made up of one person or more. Again the one person can be a solicitor or a team of “experts”. The “experts” are usually from medical background; social workers; solicitors; and specialist doctors ie. Mental Health/Psychiatry. Responsible for the assets of the Ward, including property. Making sure the Ward's house is not taken over by ruthless auctioneers, squatted by strangers, walked over by neighbours, gardens and house are maintained, monitoring the Ward's assets are in safe hands.

Nursing Homes

Nursing Homes are always happy to take on Wards. It is guaranteed for them to make profits from 1,500 euros up per week. This does not include their own GP's visits and fees; nor the maintenance of the Nursing Home; nor the Hairdresser, Reflexologist, taxis ferrying to hospitals and cancelling taxis by the Nursing Home; nor gifts to people Ward may not know of etc etc. Nursing Home charge extra for diapers - note the residents do not always use the diapers or good quality clothes - these just get purchased and expenses sent into the Office of Wards of Court. The Nursing Home have the authority to say whether or not the Ward can go out for an hour or a day. In this case, the family were refused a number of times because, quote “you are not to be trusted”, unquote. When the weather was good, “it is too hot to take him out ” or if the weather was cold, “ it is too cold to take him out ”. One of the family member was phoned a few times to purchase two new pairs of shoes for the Ward and another time to take the Ward to his hospital trips - because the Nursing Home was short-staffed and no time to take the Ward out.

The GP

The GP for the Nursing Home does not have to spend any time with the Ward. When he/she does, they are always having “a meeting” behind closed doors. In this case, the Ward asked the GP several times for his wish to be removed from the Office of Wards of Court. The GP declined the Ward said anything and indicated “he is not of sound mind”. The GP said he did not have the power of authority to remove the Ward from the Wards of Court and referred the family and Ward to the Office of Wards of Court who said it was the Solicitor. The Solicitor said it was the GP. Then the GP said it was the Nursing Home
